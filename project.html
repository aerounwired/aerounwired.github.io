    • Drone

    Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) are more properly known as Drone. Basically, drone is a flying robot. Working in combination with GPS, the flying machine may be remotely controlled or can fly autonomously by software controlled flight plans in their embedded systems. Drones are most often used in military services. However, it is also used for weather monitoring, firefighting, search and rescue, surveillance and traffic monitoring etc.

    • Aim

      To study the design and modifications of different kinds of drones specifically multi-rotors also known as multi-coptors which has simpler rotor mechanics required for flight control.

    • Approach

      Drone technology is constantly evolving as new innovation and big investment are bringing more advanced drones.aim is to Identify these new problms and accordingly design and modify quadcoptors. Some of the modification includes adding gps, Obstacle Detection And Collision Avoidance Technology and adding different sensors to gesture controlled drones.

    Vertical Landing And Take-Off ( VTOL )

    • Aim

      To develop a VTOL (Vertical Take Off and Landing) RC aircraft and test out the flight characteristics as compared to a traditional fixed wing RC aircraft.

    • Approach

      The approach includes design of the RC VTOL aircraft based on previous models, fabrication of final design and fuselage balancing, and piloting of the VTOL after adding the wing.

    Vertical Take Off and Landing aims to eliminate the need for runways for aircrafts. In theory, it should offer better maneuverability and more fuel efficiency than an aircraft using a runway. However, a truly efficient model has yet to be developed which realizes this. While there exist several helicopters in the world, there does not exist a convention winged aircraft with VTOL capabilities.

    Thrust Vectoring

    We all enjoy lighting up rocket crackers right..!?But we all just focus on the fact that it has to go up..just up..no matter which direction but it has to be in the sky for some time...But have you ever wondered about the fact that if we can't control these small cracker rockets, how do they controll the real rockets and missiles? What happens if we fail to control its direction? How do they do it? Well after reading this you will know how .
    thrust vectors as its name suggest is done by controlling the direction of thrust from its engine. When it turns in a direction the rocket also change its direction accordingly just like a fish uses its tail.

    • Aim

      To manipulate the direction of the thrust from a rockets engine or motor in order to control it's attitude or angular velocity

    • Approach

      The thrust vectoring system is an Arduino based system which uses the inputs from the gyro sensor to move the rocket motor using the metal gear servos for X-Y oriented movement thus determining the direction of the rocket. The two servos are attached to the rocket motor which is placed independent to the rocket body; thus determining the X and Y coordinates

    Fin Stabilzation

    • Aim

      To allow a rocket to maintain is orientation and intended flight path by providing a restoring torque caused by air drag

    • Approach

      By using an Arduino based system and a gyro sensor the vertical tilt of the rocket can be estimated. And servo controlled fins can be used to provide the necessary torque required to correct the rockets trajectory.

    Control systems for rockets keep a rocket stable in flight and steer it. Small rockets usually require only a stabilizing control system. Large rockets, such as the ones that launch satellites into orbit, require a system that not only stabilizes the rocket but also enables it to change course while in flight.
    Fin stabilizers are an another way to stabilize the rockets. These are generally alingned in the direction of motion. But when the rocket changes its direction due to air flow , the air will strike these fin stabilizers and thus a component of this force is used to rotate the rocket back into the desired direction and thus stabilizing it.

    Landing Gear

    With the fast development of the world aerospace, the design and manufacture of landing gear are becoming hot topics nowadays. The landing gear is an important bearing support structure of aircraft, during the safe landing process of aircraft, larger load and stronger vibration between the aircraft and the ground will be produced, the buffer system of aircraft would absorb and consume huge impact energy, so the quality of the landing gear buffer system influence the safety and comfort of aircraft directly.

    • Aim

      Designing an efficient and highly effective undercarriage system, that can be modified and used for any RC aircraft.

    • Approach

      We are learning the basic designing criterion for landing gear, calculations involved, prominent designs, metallurgy etc.
