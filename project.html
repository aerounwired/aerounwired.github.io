    Thrust Vectoring

    Source:Wikipedia

    We all enjoy lighting up rocket crackers right..!?But we all just focus on the fact that it has to go up..just up..no matter which direction but it has to be in the sky for some time...But have you ever wondered about the fact that if we can't control these small cracker rockets, how do they controll the real rockets and missiles? What happens if we fail to control its direction? How do they do it? Well after reading this you will know how .
    thrust vectors as its name suggest is done by controlling the direction of thrust from its engine. When it turns in a direction the rocket also change its direction accordingly just like a fish uses its tail.

    • Aim

      To manipulate the direction of the thrust from a rockets engine or motor in order to control it's attitude or angular velocity

    • Approach

      The thrust vectoring system is an Arduino based system which uses the inputs from the gyro sensor to move the rocket motor using the metal gear servos for X-Y oriented movement thus determining the direction of the rocket. The two servos are attached to the rocket motor which is placed independent to the rocket body; thus determining the X and Y coordinates

    Fin Stabilzation

    • Aim

      To allow a rocket to maintain is orientation and intended flight path by providing a restoring torque caused by air drag

    • Approach

      By using an Arduino based system and a gyro sensor the vertical tilt of the rocket can be estimated. And servo controlled fins can be used to provide the necessary torque required to correct the rockets trajectory.

    Source:Wikipedia

    Control systems for rockets keep a rocket stable in flight and steer it. Small rockets usually require only a stabilizing control system. Large rockets, such as the ones that launch satellites into orbit, require a system that not only stabilizes the rocket but also enables it to change course while in flight.
    Fin stabilizers are an another way to stabilize the rockets. These are generally alingned in the direction of motion. But when the rocket changes its direction due to air flow , the air will strike these fin stabilizers and thus a component of this force is used to rotate the rocket back into the desired direction and thus stabilizing it.

    Ornithoper

    Source:Wikipedia

    An ornithopter is an aircraft that flies by flapping its wings. It is designed by imitating the flapping wing flight of birds. An effective ornithopter will have wings capable of generating both thrust (forward propeling force) and lift (force that keeps the craft airbone). These forces must be strong enough to counter the effects of drag and weight of the craft. Practical applications capitalize on the resemblance to birds or insects. They can be used for military applications such as aerial reconnaissance without alerting the enemies that they are under surveillance .

    • Approach

      Fabricating a model and analyzing the model using software

    Autogyro

    Source:Wikipedia

    An autogyro also known as a gyroplane or gyrocopter, is a type of rotorcraft that uses an unpowered rotor in free autorotation to develop lift. Forward thrust is provided independently, typically by an engine-driven propeller. While similar to a helicopter rotor in appearance, the autogyro's rotor must have air flowing across the rotor disc to generate rotation, and the air flows upwards through the rotor disc rather than down. It actually uses pusher-puller configuration. Genarally autogyros are not capable of vertical takeoff (except in a strong headwind). It is perfect for customs coast-watch, surveillance of steep or very large borders, drug-fighting strategies and military missions. The gyrocopter is the ideal aircraft for this task due to its high visibility (semi-faired tandem cabin) and its wide range of flight possibilities (hover, slow and fast flight, slide). It shows its versatility in agriculture spraying, control of livestock farms. It is also used in aerial photography and filming.

    • Aim

      Designing an Autogyro by understanding the basic concept involved

    • Approach

      Initial phase includes design and modelling an autogyro after which fabrication will begin using suitable materials.

    Tricopter

    • Aim

      To design a tricopter capable of carrying out surveillance activities and other engaing tasks

    • Approach

      The approach includes design of the tricopter and fabrication wih the materials available in the current market.

    A tricopter is similar to a helicopter except that it has three vertical rotors as exposed to a heli's one ( or two in the case of coaxial). It is a remote controlled aircraft .The design of a tricopter includes three motors, one of which is vectored. A tricopter consists of three motors, three controllers, one servo and four gyros. Three rotors, which generate a greater buoyancy are used for the movement and control. Three generally equal booms are attached on the trunk of the tricopter the distance is 120 degrees to each other.A gyroscope is used for stabilization.It stabilizes the yaw axis and prevents unwanted rotation.The beauty of the design is that three ordinary outtrunners direct driving ordinary propellers can be used. Four helicopter gyros makes the tricopter super stable. And to make it yaw a servo tilts the back motor. A tricopter stabilizes automatically. The advantages of the tricopter are they are small ,durable, stable flight with unique designs, very agile due to swivel rear engines and have a larger front field of view of camera
